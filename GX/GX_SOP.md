# Guest Experience (GX) - Standard Operating Procedure

## 1. General On-Site Logistics
Guest Experience is the physical manifestation of our brand narrative. Every interaction must feel frictionless, intentional, and perfectly sequenced.

### 1.1 Pre-Arrival Protocol
- **Guest Rosters:** Must be finalized 48 hours prior to arrival.
- **Atmosphere Preparation:** Environmental controls (lighting, temperature, scent) must be activated exactly 2 hours prior to the first guest's arrival. 
- **Staffing Alignment:** All GX representatives must be fully briefed on VIP attendees, dietary restrictions, and specific experiential touchpoints.

### 1.2 The "Invisible Hand" Rule
Service should be perceived but rarely seen. Refilling, clearing, and directing should happen seamlessly. GX staff should anticipate needs without requiring the guest to explicitly ask.

---

## 2. The "Fairytale Chapter" Execution

The **Fairytale Chapter** is our elite execution standard reserved *exclusively* for micro-weddings defined as having **30 guests or fewer**. It is designed to be an intensely intimate, highly curated, narrative-driven experience.

### 2.1 Guest Threshold
To maintain the required level of intimacy and bespoke attention, the 30-guest limit is absolute. Exceeding this boundary automatically defaults the event back to standard modular service.

### 2.2 Sequence of Events
- **The Prologue (Arrival):** Guests arrive at a secondary, isolated location (e.g., a garden path, a historic library). They are served a signature archaic cocktail or tea before being led to the main staging area. No immediate sight of the ceremony space is allowed.
- **The Union (Ceremony):** Conducted in deep intimacy. Seating must be perfectly circular or uniquely non-traditional (e.g., lounge furniture, mismatched vintage chairs) heavily focusing attention on the couple.
- **The Feast (Dinner):** A singular long table execution is required. Dining is plated and coursed out over a minimum of 2.5 hours to emulate a historic banquet. 
- **The Epilogue (Departure):** Each guest receives a personalized, written artifact related to the couple's narrative before their departure.

### 2.3 Environmental Requirements
- **Audio:** A stark shift from ambient environmental noise during arrival, to a focused, isolated sound bath or live acoustic string arrangement during the ceremony.
- **Lighting:** Dimmed. Candlelight must outnumber electric light sources 3-to-1 in all indoor spaces post-sunset.
