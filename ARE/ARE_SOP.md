# Acquisitions (ARE) - Standard Operating Procedure

## 1. Purpose
This SOP details the standardized criteria for evaluating, grading, and securing underpriced or wholesale properties primarily intended for rehabilitation.

## 2. Target Property Profile
The Acquisitions team specifically targets properties demonstrating significant value-add potential due to aesthetic degradation, structural neglect, or highly motivated sellers. 

### 2.1 Essential Criteria
- **Status:** Off-market, pre-foreclosure, REO, or listed on market for > 90 days.
- **Condition:** "Heavy Cosmetic" to "Full Gut." Properties requiring solely light cosmetic work are generally deprioritized due to smaller margins.
- **Location:** Emerging neighborhoods or historic districts showing documented gentrification metrics or municipal revitalization grants.

## 3. Evaluation Metrics

All properties must pass the following multi-tiered evaluation before an offer is materialized.

### 3.1 The 70% Rule Validation
The core tenant of our financial assessment revolves around the Maximum Allowable Offer (MAO).
- **Formula:** `MAO = (ARV * 0.70) - Estimated Repair Costs`
- *ARV (After Repair Value)* must be supported by a minimum of three comparable sales (Comps) within a 0.5-mile radius, sold within the last 90 days.

### 3.2 Rehabilitation Estimate (RE) Framework
A rapid estimate must be generated utilizing historical cost-per-square-foot data before an on-site walkthrough.
- **Light RE:** $25 - $35 / sq ft (Paint, flooring, fixture updates)
- **Moderate RE:** $45 - $60 / sq ft (Kitchen/Bath gut, roof, HVAC)
- **Heavy RE:** $75+ / sq ft (Structural changes, foundation, full MEPs)

### 3.3 The "Untapped Narrative" Metric
Given our organizational structure, properties possess higher value to us if they retain distinct architectural history (e.g., Victorian styling, original masonry, historical provenance). 
Properties scoring highly on the *Untapped Narrative Metric* may be granted up to a 5% variance allowance on the standard 70% rule, pending executive approval.

## 4. Offer Submission Protocol
1. **Initial Due Diligence:** Desktop review of title records, zoning laws, and ARV confirmation.
2. **On-Site Assessment:** Verify structural integrity (roof, foundation, mechanicals) to dial in the RE Framework.
3. **Drafting:** Submit cash offer utilizing standard state-approved wholesale or purchase agreements with a required 10-day inspection contingency.
4. **Negotiation:** Initial offer should be positioned 10-15% below our calculated MAO to allow margin for seller negotiation.
