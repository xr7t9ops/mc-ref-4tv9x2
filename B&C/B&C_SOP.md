# Brand & Creative (B&C) - Standard Operating Procedure

## 1. Core Aesthetic: Dark Academia
All Brand & Creative outputs must rigorously adhere to the **Dark Academia** aesthetic. This encompasses visual assets, written collateral, tone of voice, and overall experiential design.

### 1.1 Elements of Dark Academia
- **Color Palette:** Deep, moody tones are required. Primary colors should include rich burgundies, forest greens, midnight blues, deep charcoals, and sepia. Avoid neon, pastel, or overly bright shades.
- **Typography:** Utilize serif typefaces for body copy and headings (e.g., Garamond, Baskerville, Playfair Display). Typography should feel historic, academic, and slightly worn.
- **Visual Texture:** incorporate textures simulating aged paper, leather, tweed, or shadowed architecture.
- **Imagery:** Focus on academic settings, old libraries, classical art, gothic architecture, shadows, statues, candlelight, and vintage books.
- **Tone:** The voice should be intelligent, slightly mysterious, deeply romantic, melancholic but refined, and articulate.

## 2. Client Narratives
The narrative provided to our clients is paramount to establishing the pure experiential depth of our service. However, it must remain curated, focused, and immersive. 

### 2.1 The 3-5 Page Limitation
**STRICT ENFORCEMENT:** No client narrative document may bypass *5 pages* or drop below *3 pages*. 
- **Minimum Threshold (3 pages):** Ensures adequate world-building and character or event depth.
- **Maximum Threshold (5 pages):** Preserves the mystery, sustains reading engagement, and prevents bloat.

### 2.2 Formatting Requirements
- **Title Page:** Must include the client name, date of narrative inception, and an overarching title written in Latin or archaic phrasing.
- **Body Text:** Formatted to resemble typed script or classical published novels.
- **Language:** Dense, evocative, deeply descriptive but moving with intent. Avoid conversational, modern slang.

## 3. Review Process
Before any collateral or narrative is dispatched to a client, it must undergo a peer review specifically targeting aesthetic alignment. Any deviation from the Dark Academia core must be revised prior to finalization.
